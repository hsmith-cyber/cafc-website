Article VElection (Last Bylaws Revision 2017)

Section 1: Time and Place

  • a)Election of officers shall take place in odd and even (or staggered) years at the annual BCOC conference.
  • b)Elections of the Chairperson, Recording Secretary, Financial Secretary, and Parliamentarian shall take place in odd years. (See Constitution & By-Laws for position duties, Article IV.)
  • c)Elections of the Vice Chairperson, Treasurer, and Correspondence Secretary shall take place in even years. (See Constitution & By-Laws for position duties, Article IV.)
  • d)Executive Board Recommendation for Exception to the Election Process: The elections of the Chairperson, Recording Secretary, Financial Secretary, and Parliamentarian shall take place this year to ensure the continuity of organization operations.

Eligibility for Office

To be eligible for office, a candidate must have been an individual or retired member in good standing for at least three (3) consecutive years (2024, 2025, 2026). Service must have been immediately prior to the announcement of intention to run for office.

Those desiring to run for office must be financial and in good standing at least thirty (30) days prior to the start of the annual conference to be eligible to vote in the election.

Members in good standing wishing to be elected to office shall submit their name and the office being sought. They will submit a profile of their qualifications, high-resolution headshot, and a position statement (not to exceed 250 words) to the Elections Committee in writing by April 15 July 1, 2026 of the year they are seeking election. Nominations will be accepted starting no later than May 1, 2026 due to the delays.

The Corresponding/Recording Secretaries shall ensure that candidate profiles, headshot, and position statements are disseminated to all members of the organization no later than May 15 July 6, 2026, and also placed on the website within 5 days after submission in coordination with the Elections Chair.

Voting Eligibility

  • Member in good standing with BCOC.
  • Member must be duly registered for the meeting (payment of registration fee). His/her name must appear on the roll of the treasurer.
  • Each member of the meeting is entitled to one vote and only one vote. (Voting instructions below.)

The election committee will certify the "official results" of the vote and present them to the membership. Thank you for your participation, and we look forward to seeing you in Atlanta, GA.
