About Chief Brewton

Chief T. Pierre Brewton is the Fire Chief of the City of Spartanburg (SC) Fire Department, appointed in March 2024, and serves as the Education Coordinator for the Black Chief Officers Committee (BCOC). He began his career with the City of Spartanburg in 1988, transitioned to the Fire Department in 1994, and rose to Assistant Fire Chief — the highest-ranking minority member in the department's history at that time. He previously served four years as Fire Chief of the Albemarle (NC) Fire Department before returning to lead Spartanburg. He is credentialed through the Center for Public Safety Excellence as Chief Fire Officer and Chief Training Officer, and is a graduate of Texas A&M's Fire Service Chief Executive Fire Officer Program and the IAFC Fire Service Executive Development Institute.

Keynote

Leadership Through Adversity: Navigating The Fire Service as Black Chief Officers

This curriculum explores the lived experiences, challenges, and leadership realities faced by Black Chief Officers in the fire service. It examines historical and cultural barriers, the impact of adversity on leadership development, and the responsibility of chief officers to shape equitable organizational culture. Through reflection and practical strategies, the program emphasizes mentorship, accountability, and intentional leadership to strengthen the profession.
