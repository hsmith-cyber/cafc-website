About Chief John Butler

Chief John S. Butler brings a 33+ year career dedicated to the fire service, driven by a commitment to advancing life safety standards and protecting those who serve. He is Fire Chief, Paramedic, and Fire Marshal for the Fairfax County (Virginia) Fire and Rescue Department, a role he has held since September 2018, and previously served as Fire Chief for the Howard County (Maryland) Department of Fire and Rescue Services. His experience leading two large metropolitan fire departments — combined with his roles as the 147th President of the International Association of Fire Chiefs (IAFC) and President of the Metropolitan Fire Chiefs Association — has allowed him to champion community risk reduction and occupational health, safety, and wellness on a global scale. He believes in the power of international partnerships to modernize fire codes and save lives. Chief Butler is a graduate of the Fire Service Executive Development Institute (FSEDI). Beyond the fire service, he is also a voice actor who contributed to an Emmy-nominated documentary, bringing the same passion for storytelling to the booth as he does to public safety leadership. A retired United States Marine — Semper Fidelis — his keynote directly addresses the conference theme, examining the strategic leadership required to navigate current challenges facing the fire service.

Keynote

The Fire Service in 2026: Forging the Future

This keynote directly addresses the conference theme, examining the evolving landscape of fire and emergency services and the strategic leadership required to navigate current and future challenges. Chief Butler draws on extensive executive leadership experience to outline a vision for where the fire service must go and how today's leaders can prepare their organizations for what lies ahead.
