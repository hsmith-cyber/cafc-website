Day 1

Monday, August 3

Vision and Legacy
KEYNOTE Developing Your Fire Service Career
Chief Joel Baker
Austin Fire Department
Developing a fire service career requires a strategic approach blending continuous training, higher education, and specialized certifications. This course outlines several pillars of professional development designed to help firefighters preparing for leadership positions within the fire service. Topics include networking strategies, National Fire Academy pathways, professional organizations, and resume building for executive roles.
Learning Objectives
Identify steps for developing a Career Fire Plan for Advancement. Create a networking group within and outside the fire service. Outline requirements for a professional resume and bio for leadership applications. Determine readiness for applying for a fire chief position.
About the PresenterChief Baker has presented at TEEX Leadership Symposium, Georgia Fire Chiefs Association, Texas Fire Chiefs Association, and the Black Chief Officers Committee.
KEYNOTE Leadership Through Adversity: Navigating The Fire Service as Black Chief Officers
Chief T. Pierre Brewton
City of Spartanburg (SC) Fire Department
This curriculum explores the lived experiences, challenges, and leadership realities faced by Black Chief Officers in the fire service. It examines historical and cultural barriers, the impact of adversity on leadership development, and the responsibility of chief officers to shape equitable organizational culture. Through reflection and practical strategies, the program emphasizes mentorship, accountability, and intentional leadership to strengthen the profession.
Learning Objectives
Recognize systematic and cultural challenges Black Fire Officers face in leadership, promotion, and organizational culture. Understand how adversity shapes leadership style, decision-making, and resilience. Apply strategies for equitable leadership and mentorship that support inclusion. Develop actionable steps to improve departmental culture and promote fairness at all levels.

Day 2

Tuesday, August 4

Challenges and Innovation
KEYNOTE "But Still I Lead"
Dr. I. David Daniels, PhD, CSD, VPS
Founder & CEO, ID2 Solutions
This keynote explores the lived experience of Black executive leadership in the United States. Dr. Daniels speaks candidly about the challenges and cumulative trauma that often accompany leadership in environments shaped by inequity, the importance of recognizing and naming that trauma, and the healing practices that allow leaders to move forward with clarity, strength, and purpose.
Learning Objectives
Recognize and name the cumulative trauma associated with executive leadership. Identify healing practices that restore clarity, strength, and purpose. Understand how personal healing creates healthier organizational environments. Develop frameworks for sustaining leadership resilience.
About the PresenterAuthor of "Psychosocial Hazards Are Real!" Book signing available during breaks.
The Top 10 Moves to Use AI to Transform Fire & Emergency Services
Chief Steven Carter
City of Sunrise Fire Rescue
AI is an active force reshaping the landscape in which every department operates. Private sector AI investment is projected to reach $700 billion in 2026. The federal government has released a national AI Action Plan and rolled out NERIS, a new AI-enabled national fire data platform. This session provides chief officers with a structured, actionable framework for leading AI adoption, grounded in Everett Rogers' Diffusion of Innovation theory and the Technology-Organization-Environment adoption framework.
Learning Objectives
Describe the current scale of AI investment and its implications for fire services. Identify three dimensions of AI adoption and assess departmental readiness. Explain the Diffusion of Innovation model and locate the fire service's position. Discuss strategic moves to initiate AI adoption within 30 days.
The KMP Mindset: Building Leadership Teams in Uncertain Times
Dr. Kim McNair
Kim McNair Productions LLC
Uncertain times demand a different level of leadership. This session explores how to build and sustain high-performing leadership teams using the KMP Mindset: Knowledge, Money, and Power. Participants will gain strategies to strengthen decision-making, maximize resources, and lead with authority and purpose.
Learning Objectives
Apply the KMP Mindset to lead effectively in uncertain times. Strengthen decision-making under pressure. Build and align high-performing leadership teams. Lead with confidence, clarity, and influence.
You Have Received a "Vote of No Confidence" — Now What?
Chief Joel Baker
Austin Fire Department
This session reviews a vote of no confidence in the fire service: a formal, usually non-binding vote by a firefighter's union expressing lack of faith in leadership. Participants will learn to identify contributing factors, manage the process, and lead their department through it effectively.
Learning Objectives
Identify factors that should be considered for a vote of no confidence. Determine what is needed to manage a vote of no confidence. Address issues that may lead to a vote of no confidence. Learn do's and don'ts of leading during this process.
Workshop: Care Bias in Emergency Services
Chief Mike Scott & Chief Calvin Brown
Grand Fire Protection District
Care bias in EMS refers to the unintentional or intentional influence of factors like race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, and other social determinants on the quality of care that patients receive. This interactive workshop offers strategies to reduce disparities and highlights best practices for promoting equitable care delivery.
Learning Objectives
Define care bias and how it manifests in emergency medical services. Recognize the impact on patient outcomes including disparities in treatment and misdiagnosis. Examine root causes including cultural stereotypes, personal biases, and systemic inequalities. Identify practical strategies to combat care bias using standardized assessment tools and cultural competence training.
Strategic Grant Funding for the Modern Fire Service
Chief Emanuel Washington Jr.
Orlando Fire Department
In today's resource-constrained environment, fire service leaders must think beyond traditional funding streams. This session provides a practical, executive-level approach to securing and managing grant funding through AFG, SAFER, SHSGP, and UASI programs. This is not just about writing grants: it is about leveraging funding as a strategic leadership tool.
Learning Objectives
Identify key fire service funding programs and their strategic uses. Align departmental needs with grant priorities and scoring criteria. Develop stronger grant narratives that improve funding success rates. Apply strategies for post-award management, compliance, and sustainability.
About the PresenterChief Washington has presented at IAFC HazMat Conference, FFCA Executive Development Seminar, National Fire Academy EFO Leadership Forum, and City of Orlando Command Symposiums.
"Shaping the Standard" — Panel: Procurement Strategies (Apparatus)
Chief Ron Skrine
Battalion Chief (Administration), Valdosta Fire Department
This panel tackles the strategic side of fire service procurement — developing specifications for apparatus, PPE, SCBA, and uniforms; managing vendor relationships; aligning purchases with long-range city goals; and building replacement cycles that hold up under budget scrutiny. Panelists share lessons learned from multi-million-dollar procurements and the organizational planning that turns a purchase into a standard.
Learning Objectives
Translate long-range departmental goals into apparatus, PPE, and equipment specifications. Structure vendor management and procurement processes that withstand scrutiny. Align capital purchases with ISO, accreditation, and operational readiness priorities. Apply lessons from modernization projects such as personnel management systems, inventory control, and keyless entry.
About the PresenterChief Ronald D. Skrine Sr., MA, is Battalion Chief of Administration with the Valdosta Fire Department, where he has served more than 26 unbroken years, including 12 years on the Executive Command Staff. He is an Adjunct Fire Science & Emergency Management Instructor at Purdue University and has served as BCOC's Educational Liaison since 2019. Certifications include Fire Officer IV, Incident Safety Officer, and Fire Instructor I & II.

The Future of Leadership
KEYNOTE The Seven Pillars of Modern Leadership
Dr. Hezedean Smith, Fire Chief (Ret.)
BCOC Chair / GESCG CEO
The Seven Pillars of Leadership are adaptable to any leadership ecosystem. This presentation connects leadership principles to everyday fire service functions including budgeting, personnel management, and interdepartmental coordination, demonstrating how disciplined, ethical leadership strengthens operations and community confidence.
Learning Objectives
Translate the NEMSMA Seven Pillars into fire department leadership across task management, leading others, innovation, and social responsibility. Implement components to enhance teamwork and resilience. Foster continuous learning and data-driven performance improvement. Demonstrate adaptive decision-making and psychological safety within teams.
KEYNOTE Healing While Leading: Mental Health and Resilience for Black Executives
Dr. I. David Daniels (Moderator)
ID2 Solutions with Atlanta-based Mental Health Professionals
This panel features mental health professionals who specialize in supporting individuals and organizations through trauma, healing, and transformation. Their expertise complements the conference's leadership focus and offers attendees practical, culturally grounded insights into sustaining well-being and leadership effectiveness.
Learning Objectives
Understand the intersection of mental health and executive leadership. Identify culturally grounded healing practices for Black leaders. Develop strategies for sustaining personal well-being while leading organizations. Build frameworks for creating psychologically safe organizational cultures.
From Academy to Degree: A Workforce Model for the Modern Fire Service
Dr. Kellie McElroy-Hooper & Chief Antonio Burden
Augusta Technical College / Augusta Fire Department
What if every hour of fire academy training also moved firefighters toward a degree and a leadership position without adding cost or time? This presentation highlights a pioneering partnership in Georgia that serves as a national model for aligning fire academy training with higher education through Credit for Prior Learning.
Learning Objectives
Examine how career departments can align training with academic pathways. Examine how Credit for Prior Learning accelerates degree attainment. Assess how integrated models strengthen recruitment, improve retention, and expand access. Develop a practical, scalable implementation strategy.
About the PresenterDr. McElroy-Hooper is Dean at Augusta Technical College with three decades of public safety experience. Chief Burden oversees an ISO Class 1 department with over 300 personnel and is a U.S. Marine Corps veteran.
From LeaderShip to Leader of ShipS™
Dr. Joseph Danao II, Ed.D.
Eastern Connecticut EMS Council
This course focuses on practical life lessons that develop leadership at every organizational level. Emphasizing deliberate growth, strong values, and learning from challenge and failure, it equips fire and EMS professionals with the mindset and skills needed to lead with confidence, purpose, and effectiveness.
Learning Objectives
Evaluate current tactical and operational leadership traits. Analyze three learning theories using Jerome Bruner's constructivist approach. Apply principles of strategic thinking used by senior military and civilian leaders. Create a roadmap for senior leadership incorporating Executive Leadership Theory.
About the PresenterDr. Danao brings 42 years of Firefighter/EMS service, 33 years of military service including deployment to Afghanistan (Bronze Star Medal), and a Doctor of Education in Healthcare Executive Leadership (2026).
Financial "Hot Wash" (Protecting Your Legacy)
Chief Queen Anunay & Chief Wendell Catlet
Las Vegas Fire & Rescue
This session helps first responders make informed decisions regarding payroll contributions, real estate investments, life insurance options, and retirement planning. First responders are often the main providers within their family structure, requiring financial strategies beyond the average salary.
Learning Objectives
Define your legacy and long-term financial goals. Understand payroll contributions: save now, pay later. Learn to use life insurance as an investment vehicle. Explore real estate investment strategies. Develop your exit strategy with comprehensive retirement planning.
From Strategy to Execution: Turning Vision into Results
Chief Demond Simmons
Oakland Fire Department
This course explores how to translate strategic vision into executable plans, concrete initiatives, and measurable outcomes. Participants will examine why well-intended strategies often fail in implementation and develop practical tools to close the gap between plans and results.
Learning Objectives
Discuss common strategic goals across fire service organizations. Review processes and technologies that assist with executing strategic plans. Ensure collaboration and accountability in alignment with the strategic plan. Understand the importance of execution and performance-based decision-making.

KEYNOTE The Fire Service in 2026: Forging the Future
Chief John Butler
Metro Chiefs Chair
This keynote directly addresses the conference theme, examining the evolving landscape of fire and emergency services and the strategic leadership required to navigate current and future challenges. Chief Butler draws on extensive executive leadership experience to outline a vision for where the fire service must go and how today's leaders can prepare their organizations for what lies ahead.
Learning Objectives
Assess the current state of the fire service and identify emerging challenges. Develop strategic approaches to organizational transformation. Build frameworks for sustainable leadership in evolving environments. Align departmental priorities with national trends and community expectations.
KEYNOTE Global Perspectives on Executive Fire Service Leadership
Dr. Reggie Freeman
This keynote examines fire service leadership through an international lens, drawing on global best practices and cross-cultural leadership strategies. Dr. Freeman explores how executive fire service leaders can learn from international models, adapt proven approaches from other countries, and position their organizations within a broader global context of public safety innovation.
Learning Objectives
Examine international best practices in fire service leadership and operations. Identify cross-cultural leadership strategies applicable to U.S. fire departments. Analyze global trends shaping the future of emergency services. Develop a broader perspective on executive leadership that incorporates international insights.
Revenue Generation
William Bryant
Mobile Area Council, Scouting America
This session focuses on building a diverse, sustainable funding model through a combination of philanthropic gifts, unit sales, and fee-for-service activities. The approach emphasizes a planned system where revenue is strategically aligned with program goals to ensure long-term organizational sustainability.
Learning Objectives
Recognize the importance of volunteer and professional relationships in fundraising. Conduct and review a SWOT Analysis for your organization. Understand how to cultivate major gifts and the four types of asking styles. Participate in a donor meeting simulation. Create a strategy memo and prepare to close the deal.
About the PresenterWilliam Bryant has presented at the Association of Fundraising Professionals International Conference, Cary School of Philanthropy at Auburn University, and the Community Foundation of South Alabama.
Financial Access and Acumen
Jere Hawkins
This session equips fire service leaders with practical financial literacy tools and strategies for improving their personal and organizational financial health. Topics include understanding financial instruments, building credit, accessing capital, and developing the financial acumen needed for effective departmental budget management and personal wealth building.
Learning Objectives
Understand key financial instruments and their applications. Develop strategies for improving personal and organizational financial health. Build financial literacy skills relevant to fire service leadership. Apply financial planning principles to departmental budget management.
Developing an Explorer Program
Carlos Coronado
Scouting America
This session provides a practical roadmap for establishing and growing a Fire Explorer program within your department. Explorer programs serve as a pipeline for recruiting diverse, community-connected future firefighters while providing young people with mentorship, career exposure, and leadership development opportunities.
Learning Objectives
Understand the structure and benefits of a Fire Explorer program. Identify steps to launch or expand an Explorer post within your department. Develop recruitment strategies that reach diverse youth populations. Build partnerships between fire departments and community organizations.
Marketing Your Vision
Maisha Land
Effective leadership requires the ability to communicate and market your strategic vision to stakeholders, elected officials, community members, and your own personnel. This session covers branding, messaging, social media strategy, and public relations techniques that help fire service leaders build support for their initiatives and elevate their department's profile.
Learning Objectives
Develop a clear and compelling vision statement for your department. Create effective messaging strategies for diverse audiences. Leverage social media and digital platforms to build community engagement. Apply marketing principles to build support for departmental initiatives.
