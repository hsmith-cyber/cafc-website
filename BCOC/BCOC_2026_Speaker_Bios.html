Keynote Speaker
Chief Joel Baker
Fire Chief, Austin Fire Department
Chief Joel Baker serves as Fire Chief of the Austin Fire Department. His sessions focus on developing a strategic fire service career — blending continuous training, higher education, certifications, networking, and professional organizations — and on leading a department through a formal vote of no confidence, covering the responsibilities of the fire chief, city manager, and mayor before, during, and after such a vote. He has presented at the TEEX Leadership Symposium, Georgia Fire Chiefs Association, Texas Fire Chiefs Association, and the Black Chief Officers Committee.

Keynote Speaker
Dr. I. David Daniels, PhD, CSD, VPS
Founder & CEO, ID2 Solutions
Dr. I. David Daniels is a global thought leader in occupational health and psychosocial safety. As Founder and CEO of ID2 Solutions, LLC, he brings over four decades of frontline and executive leadership—including four Fire Chief appointments—to the challenge of protecting workers from psychosocial hazards. His doctoral research (Capitol Technology University, 2022) and his book Psychosocial Hazards Are Real! provide frameworks for understanding the hidden risks that undermine worker health and organizational culture. He serves as Chief Safety Officer and board member for multiple advocacy organizations, focusing on psychosocial hazard regulation, trauma-informed accountability, equity in occupational health and safety, and strategic coalition leadership.

Keynote Speaker
Chief John Alston
Fire Chief (Ret.), Vice-Chair BCOC
Chief John Alston Jr. brings more than 41 years of fire service experience and a people-centered leadership philosophy. He serves as Executive Coach and Trainer for the Fire Officer Trust (since October 2018) and is a certified John Maxwell Coach and Trainer, supporting business leaders, first responders, law enforcement, and emergency management leaders. Chief Alston served as Fire Chief of the New Haven (CT) Fire Department from October 2016 through his recent retirement, becoming the first fire chief in 155 years hired from outside the department. Before New Haven, he spent nearly 16 years with the Jersey City (NJ) Fire Department — rising to 1st Battalion Chief, and previously serving as Deputy Chief of Special Operations and Chief of Training, where he led all training for a "Metro" fire department and directed fire suppression and disaster planning across high-traffic assets including the Holland Tunnel, Ellis Island, and Liberty State Park. From 2012 to 2018 he also served as a Master Instructor with the International Association of Fire Fighters (IAFF). Chief Alston is Vice-Chair of the Black Chief Officers Committee (BCOC).

Keynote Speaker
Dr. Reggie Freeman
Executive Director, Fire & EMS · NEOM · Founder, The Freeman Group
Dr. Reginald Freeman serves as Executive Director of Fire & EMS at NEOM, where his focus is on developing cutting-edge protocols and organizational strategies that enhance operational efficiency and effectiveness. His team's recent initiatives have been pivotal in improving emergency response capabilities, ensuring the safety and well-being of NEOM's residents and visitors. With over 17 years at the helm of The Freeman Group, a renowned public safety consulting firm, Dr. Freeman brings a wealth of experience to the forefront of emergency management. He previously served as Fire Chief of the Oakland (CA) Fire Department; Fire Chief and Emergency Management Director for Hartford (CT); Fire Chief for Lockheed Martin; and as a civilian Fire Chief in Iraq for the U.S. Department of Defense (2004–2008). He holds a Master's in Executive Fire Service Leadership from Grand Canyon University and a PhD in Emergency & Protective Services from Capitol Technology University. Dr. Freeman also serves as a Board Member at The Leadership Crucible Foundation, where he is deeply invested in the growth and development of leaders.

Keynote Speaker
Dr. Hezedean Smith, Fire Chief (Ret.)
BCOC Chair; President, NEMSMA; CEO, Global Emergency Services Consulting Group
Dr. Hezedean A. Smith, DM, is an accomplished and visionary consultant and fire service executive who has served in the emergency services profession for over 37 years. His credentials include CFO, CEMSO, NRP, CPM, COSS, CHSE, and EFO-EMSO. He has served as the first Black Fire Chief in Charlottesville, Virginia and Polk County, Florida, following a distinguished career of nearly 25 years with a metropolitan fire department in Florida (Orlando Fire Department), where he rose to Assistant Chief. He holds a Doctor of Management in Organizational Leadership as well as degrees in Fire Science and EMS, and is positioned to conduct strategic planning, assessments, and research aimed at improving organizations across the Caribbean and the United States. Dr. Smith has a solid track record in organizational transformation, challenging the status quo, and facilitating cutting-edge 21st-century leadership and strategic management.

Chief John Butler
Fire Chief, Fairfax County Virginia · President, Metro Chiefs
Chief John S. Butler brings a 33+ year career dedicated to the fire service, driven by a commitment to advancing life safety standards and protecting those who serve. He is Fire Chief, Paramedic, and Fire Marshal for the Fairfax County (Virginia) Fire and Rescue Department, a role he has held since September 2018.
